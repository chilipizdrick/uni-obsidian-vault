### 589

$$\begin{gather}
x^{2}y'' - 4xy' + 6y = 0 \\
\begin{bmatrix}
x = e^{t} \\
y(t(x)) \\
1 = e^{t}t'_{x} \implies t'_{x} = e^{-t} \\
y'_{x} = y'_{t}t'_{x} = y'_{t}e^{-t} \\
y''_{x} = (y''_{x}e^{-t})'_{t}t'_{x} = (y''_{t}e^{-t} - y'_{t}e^{-t})e^{-t} = e^{-2t}(y'' - y'_{t}) \\
\end{bmatrix} \\
\end{gather}$$
